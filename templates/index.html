About

At the heart of our project lies a captivating inquiry: Is there a connection between the prehistoric resting places of dinosaurs and today's fossil fuel deposits? This exploration dives into the geographical data of dinosaur fossils, tracing their ancient journey across the Earth. We contrast this with a thorough analysis of current fossil fuel production sites, seeking patterns and correlations. By bridging the gap between the Mesozoic Era and modern energy landscapes, our goal is to unearth insights that may illuminate the story of how these prehistoric giants have fueled our present-day lives, both literally and metaphorically.

Plots

Our collection of plots offers a compelling visualization of historical data and future projections. The first set of graphics maps the global distribution of dinosaur fossils, providing a detailed account of where these ancient titans once thrived. Complementing this, we present contemporary maps of oil, coal, and gas production by country, inviting analysis of any geographical overlaps with prehistoric finds.



Predictive Model

We extrapolated these trends into a predictive model for 2024, offering a forecast of energy production potential informed by the paleontological record from 2020.
