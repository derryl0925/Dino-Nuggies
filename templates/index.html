About

At the heart of our project lies a captivating inquiry: Is there a connection between the prehistoric resting places of dinosaurs and today's fossil fuel deposits? This exploration dives into the geographical data of dinosaur fossils, tracing their ancient journey across the Earth. We contrast this with a thorough analysis of current fossil fuel production sites, seeking patterns and correlations. By bridging the gap between the Mesozoic Era and modern energy landscapes, our goal is to unearth insights that may illuminate the story of how these prehistoric giants have fueled our present-day lives, both literally and metaphorically.

Plots

Our collection of plots offers a compelling visualization of historical data and future projections. The first set of graphics maps the global distribution of dinosaur fossils, providing a detailed account of where these ancient titans once thrived. Complementing this, we present contemporary maps of oil production by country, inviting analysis of any geographical overlaps with prehistoric finds.



Predictive Model

We extrapolated these trends into a predictive model for 2025, offering a forecast of energy production potential informed by the paleontological record from 2020.

Conclusion

Our investigations into the enigmatic ties between prehistoric life and present-day energy production have yielded compelling results. Upon examining the spatial relationship between dinosaur fossil sites and modern oil production areas, we've uncovered a notable correlation. The calculated Pearson Correlation Coefficient stands at 0.67, suggesting a significant positive relationship between the abundance of dinosaur remains and the volume of oil production across various regions. This intriguing connection offers a glimpse into the profound ways in which the organic matter of the past has shaped the energy resources we depend upon today.
